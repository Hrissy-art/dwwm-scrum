1.Whats is Scrum?
1.1 The framwork

Scrum helps people and teams deliver value incrementally in a collaborative way. As an agile framework, Scrum provides just enough structure for people and teams to integrate into how they work, while adding the right practices to optimize for their specific needs

1.2 The co-creators

Scrum co-creators Ken Schwaber and Jeff Sutherland

3. The Scrum events
3.1 What is a sprint

They are fixed length periods of work that last one month or less to create consistency and ensure short iterations for feedback in order to inspect and adapt both how work is done and what is being worked on. If cycles are longer, then the spirit of frequent feedback cycles can be lost. Longer Sprint may also get too complex and may increase risk. A new Sprint starts immediately after the conclusion of the previous Sprint

3.2 Whats is a spint planning

This resulting plan is created by the collaborative work of the entire Scrum Team.

3.3 What is a daily scrum? The purpose of the Daily Scrum is to inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal and adapt the Sprint Backlog as necessary, adjusting the upcoming planned work.
3.4 Whats is a sprint review?

inspect the outcome of the Sprint and determine future adaptations..

3.5 Whats is a sprint retrospective

is to plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness. The Scrum Team inspects how the last Sprint went with regards to individuals, interactions, processes, tools, and their Definition of Done

4.1 Product Backlog

the Product Backlog is an emergent, ordered list of what is needed to improve the product.

4.2 Product Goal

The Product Goal is the long-term objective for the Scrum Team.

The commitment of the Product Backlog is the Product Goal.

4.3 Sprint backlog

containes the sprint goal (why) + set of product goal items selected for the sprint (what) and increminent (how)

4.4 Sprint goal

The commitment of the Sprint Backlog is the Sprint Goal. The Sprint Goal is the single objective for the Sprint.

NB the goal must be attainable

4.5 Increment

An Increment is a concrete stepping stone toward the Product Goal

4.6 The definition of done?

The Definition of Done is the commitment by the Developers for the Increment The Definition of Done includes all of the characteristics and standards an Increment needs to meet in order to be released.
